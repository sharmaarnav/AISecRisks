Third-party AI and model risk

NISTOWASPISO 42001CISA
3 controls mapped

Description

Organisations rely on third-party AI models, APIs, or AI-powered SaaS products without adequate security assessment, creating hidden dependencies on external systems with their own vulnerabilities, data handling practices, and operational risks.

Attack scenario

An organisation embeds a third-party LLM API in a customer-facing product. The provider experiences a breach — conversation histories from all customers are exfiltrated. The organisation had no visibility into the provider's security posture.

Real-world examples

OpenAI breach exposing user conversation histories (2023). Multiple AI API providers experiencing availability incidents affecting dependent applications.

3 mapped controls

NIST
AI vendor risk assessment
GOVERN 2.1 – Supplier risk
Conduct security assessments of all AI vendors and model providers. Require SOC 2 / ISO 27001 evidence. Review data retention, training data use, and breach notification terms.
OWASP
AI SBOM and MBOM
LLM05 – Supply chain
Maintain a Software/Model Bill of Materials for all AI components. Track versions, known vulnerabilities, and end-of-support dates.
CISA
Contractual security requirements
AI supply chain risk
Include AI-specific security requirements in vendor contracts: data isolation, audit rights, incident notification timelines, and data deletion on termination.
