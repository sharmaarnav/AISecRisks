Membership inference attack

MITRE ATLASENISANISTEU AI Act
3 controls mapped

Description

An adversary determines whether a specific individual's data was included in the training dataset by observing differences in model confidence for known vs. unknown records.

Attack scenario

In a healthcare setting, an attacker confirms that a specific patient's medical record was used to train a diagnostic AI, violating privacy even without extracting the actual record content.

Real-world examples

Shokri et al. (2017) – Membership Inference Attacks Against ML Models. Demonstrated against commercial ML APIs.

3 mapped controls

MITRE
Differential privacy training
AML.T0024 – Exfil via ML APIs
Train with differential privacy guarantees (DP-SGD) to provide mathematical bounds on membership leakage. Use libraries: TensorFlow Privacy, Opacus.
ENISA
Output prediction smoothing
D.4 – Privacy attacks
Reduce model overfitting (regularisation, dropout) to decrease confidence gap between training and non-training samples.
EU AI Act
Privacy impact assessment
Art. 10 – Data governance
Conduct DPIA for AI systems processing personal data. Document membership inference risk and mitigation measures.
