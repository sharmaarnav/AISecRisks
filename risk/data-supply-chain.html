Compromised data supply chain

OWASPMITRE ATLASNISTCISA
3 controls mapped

Description

Third-party data vendors, open-source datasets, or data brokers may deliver tampered, outdated, or maliciously crafted data. Lack of provenance tracking means poisoning goes undetected.

Attack scenario

A financial AI system subscribes to a third-party market data feed. The vendor is compromised and begins injecting subtle anomalies into the feed, causing the model to make systematically biased predictions.

Real-world examples

SolarWinds-style supply chain compromise applied to ML data pipelines. Nightshade dataset poisoning.

3 mapped controls

OWASP
Data vendor vetting
LLM03 – Supply Chain
Establish supplier assurance processes for all data vendors. Require SBOMs/DBOMs. Verify data hashes on every ingestion.
MITRE
Signed data artifacts
AML.T0010 – ML supply chain
Use cryptographically signed dataset releases, maintain immutable ingestion logs, and implement automated drift detection on incoming data distributions.
CISA
Supply chain risk management
Secure AI Guidelines 2024
Apply AI-specific SCRM controls: inventory all AI data sources, assess vendor security posture, and define contractual security requirements.
