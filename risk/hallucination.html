Hallucination and factual failure

NISTEU AI ActISO 42001ENISA
3 controls mapped

Description

LLMs generate plausible-sounding but factually incorrect or entirely fabricated content with high confidence. In high-stakes domains this causes direct harm — wrong medical advice, fabricated legal citations, incorrect security guidance.

Attack scenario

A legal research AI cites six non-existent court cases in a legal brief with full citation details, case names, and fabricated holdings. A lawyer submits the brief without verification — the cases are entirely invented.

Real-world examples

Mata v. Avianca (2023) — lawyers sanctioned for submitting ChatGPT-fabricated case citations. Multiple medical AI hallucination incidents.

3 mapped controls

NIST
Grounding and RAG architecture
MEASURE 2.10 – Output accuracy
Implement Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) to ground responses in verified source documents. Apply confidence thresholding and source attribution.
EU AI Act
Human oversight requirements
Art. 13 – Transparency obligations
For high-risk applications, mandate human review of AI outputs before consequential use. Implement AI output disclaimer systems.
ISO 42001
Hallucination benchmarking
Clause 8.5 – Performance requirements
Establish and track hallucination rate benchmarks (TruthfulQA, HaluEval). Set maximum acceptable hallucination rates per use case.
