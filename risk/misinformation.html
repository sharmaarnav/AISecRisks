AI-generated misinformation and manipulation

EU AI ActNISTCISAENISA
3 controls mapped

Description

AI systems are weaponised to generate large-scale, convincing misinformation — fake news articles, fraudulent documents, deepfakes, or influence campaign content — at a speed and scale impossible by human actors alone.

Attack scenario

Threat actors use fine-tuned LLMs to generate thousands of convincing fake news articles about a company, poisoning search results and causing reputational damage before human fact-checkers can respond.

Real-world examples

GPT-4 used for political influence campaigns (2024). NCSC/CISA warnings on AI-generated election interference content.

3 mapped controls

EU AI Act
AI content labelling
Art. 50 – Transparency for GPAI
Label AI-generated content at the system level. Implement C2PA (Content Provenance and Authenticity) watermarking for images and documents.
CISA
Content authentication
AI deepfake guidance 2024
Deploy AI content detection capabilities. Apply digital content provenance standards. Train users to critically evaluate AI-generated content.
NIST
Use case restrictions
GOVERN 5 – Trustworthiness
Explicitly prohibit and detect use of AI systems for generating political content, impersonation, or synthetic media designed to deceive.
