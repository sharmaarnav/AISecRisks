Lack of explainability and transparency

NISTEU AI ActISO 42001ENISA
3 controls mapped

Description

AI systems make decisions that cannot be explained to affected individuals, auditors, or regulators. The 'black box' problem creates legal exposure, erodes trust, and prevents effective oversight and challenge of AI decisions.

Attack scenario

An AI system denies an insurance claim. The insurer cannot explain why — the model is a 300M parameter neural network with no explainability tooling. The customer challenges the decision; the insurer cannot provide a legally required explanation.

Real-world examples

GDPR 'right to explanation' enforcement actions against algorithmic decision-making. EU AI Act mandatory transparency obligations.

3 mapped controls

NIST
Model explainability tooling
MAP 2.3 – Explainability
Implement SHAP, LIME, or counterfactual explanations for high-stakes AI decisions. Publish model cards with capability and limitation documentation.
EU AI Act
User-facing explanation systems
Art. 13 – Transparency obligations
Provide meaningful explanations to affected individuals. For automated decisions, implement recourse and human review mechanisms.
ISO 42001
AI system documentation
Clause 8.4 – AI transparency
Maintain comprehensive technical documentation. Disclose AI involvement in all decisions. Enable external audit access to documentation.
