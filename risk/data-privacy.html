PII leakage in training data

NISTENISAEU AI ActISO 42001
4 controls mapped

Description

Personally identifiable information embedded in training datasets can be memorised by the model and reproduced via targeted queries. Membership inference attacks can confirm whether specific records were in training data.

Attack scenario

A medical LLM trained on patient records without proper de-identification can regurgitate real patient names, diagnoses, and addresses when prompted with partial identifying information.

Real-world examples

Samsung source code leak via ChatGPT (2023). Research demonstrating GPT-2/GPT-3 training data extraction (Carlini et al.).

4 mapped controls

ENISA
Differential privacy
Privacy threat D.4
Apply differential privacy (DP-SGD) during training, k-anonymity to raw datasets, and data minimisation — only include data strictly necessary for the AI task.
EU AI Act
Data governance requirements
Art. 10 – Data governance
High-risk AI systems must document data collection practices, purpose limitation, and bias correction measures under the EU AI Act.
NIST
PII scanning pipeline
MAP 1.5 – Privacy risk
Run PII scanning tools (e.g. Microsoft Presidio, AWS Comprehend) before ingestion. Establish retention and deletion schedules aligned to GDPR and Privacy Act 1988 (AU).
ISO 42001
AIMS data controls
Clause 8.4 – Data management
Maintain AI data inventory, apply access controls on training repositories, and document legal basis for all personal data used in training.
