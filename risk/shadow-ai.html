Shadow AI and ungoverned AI use

NISTCISAISO 42001ENISA
3 controls mapped

Description

Employees use unauthorised AI tools — consumer LLMs, AI plugins, or SaaS AI features — to process organisational data, bypassing security controls, data classification policies, and compliance requirements.

Attack scenario

An employee pastes confidential M&A documents into a consumer AI chatbot to get a summary. The data is sent to a third-party AI provider, potentially used for model training, and retained indefinitely — none of which is permitted under the organisation's data handling policy.

Real-world examples

Samsung source code leak via ChatGPT (2023). Multiple enterprises banning consumer AI tools following data leakage incidents.

3 mapped controls

NIST
AI acceptable use policy
GOVERN 1.1 – Policies
Publish an explicit AI acceptable use policy. Define approved AI tools, prohibited data types for AI processing, and consequences for policy violation.
CISA
Technical AI access controls
Secure-by-Design guidance
Deploy URL/domain filtering to block unapproved AI services on corporate networks. Apply DLP controls on AI platform traffic. Monitor for data exfiltration via AI APIs.
ISO 42001
AI tool approval process
Clause 8.1 – Operational controls
Establish a formal process for evaluating and approving AI tools. Maintain an approved AI product catalogue. Review annually or on vendor security incidents.
